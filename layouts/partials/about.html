The project is called wordgraphy.

In this project moments are captured with words.

Photography draws with light, videography draws with what we see. I draw with words.

Photography is the art of taking photographs. Wordgraphy is the art of taking wordgraphs.

 

The project is also a book. The book is called wordgraphy.

The books tells a story, of people who invent the method, of people and machines who can take wordgraphs.

It tells the story of people dependent on these wordgraphs, they try to retain by fising moments with words.

To retain what? Everything. Meaning; perhaps.

 

The project is also the process of writing the book.

Very often I will share highlights of what I wrote. With every step a new, updated edition of the book will also be published.

I will read your comments. Be kind.

 

I want to write because I receive more than what I give.

I also want to write because, when I receive, I try to keep the give forever. Sometimes what I receive is a kiss, and I want to keep the kisser; it is a blessing, and I want to keep the god.

I want to write what I receive, so I can go back to it if needed.
